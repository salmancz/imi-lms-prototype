Knowledge mapping

Knowledge audits

A knowledge audit is a systematic survey of the knowledge that exists within an organisation, where it lives, who holds it, and how it flows.

In information governance contexts, three audit moves matter more than the rest: inventory, flow, and concentration.

Inventory is a catalogue of what knowledge assets exist, documents, processes, repositories, and the implicit-but-stable patterns of practice.

Flow traces how knowledge moves: who hands what to whom, in what form, and when. Maps of flow tend to reveal bottlenecks invisible from any single role's perspective.

Concentration is the risk view: how much critical knowledge lives in how few heads. High concentration = single points of failure for an organisation.

A good practitioner-level audit answers a single question: if any one of our knowledge-holders left tomorrow, what would we no longer be able to do?
